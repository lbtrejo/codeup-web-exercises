🌲 National Parks FAQs 🌲

Learn more about America's best Natural Wonders!

Oldest Park 🎂
Yellowstone National Park is the oldest US National Park, founded in 1872
Watch your step! 🥾
Three of the 10 highest waterfalls in the world are located in Yosemite National Park!
It's a little warm in this park 🌡️
One of the hottest temperatures on Earth was recorded in 1913 in Death Valley National Park, a scorching 134 degrees F 🔥
One of Seven 🎁
The Grand Canyon, in Grand Canyon National Park, is one of the seven wonders of the world!
Gentle Giants 🌳
California's Sequoia National Park is home to the largest living single-stem tree in the world, the wonderfully named General Sherman. The tree is approximately 275 feet tall and weighs approximately 1,900 metric tons.
You can see for miles 🏞️
Mount McKinley in the Alaska Range of Denali National Park is the highest point in the national parks at 20,302 feet. ⛰️
Not one, not four, but eight! 🎊
California and Alaska each have eight national parks.
Lone Star Heights ⛰️
Guadalupe Peak, also known as Signal Peak, is the highest natural point in Texas, with an elevation of 8,751 feet above sea level. It is located in Guadalupe Mountains National Park
See them while they last ❄️
There are 25 active glaciers and more than 700 lakes of various sizes inside Glacier National Park.
Mountain Majesty 🗻
Mount Rainier is the most glaciated peak in the contiguous U.S. with 26 major glaciers.
